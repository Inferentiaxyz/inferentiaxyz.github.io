Yes data privacy must be considered when designing and deploying machine learning systems and organizations must comply with relevant laws such as the GDPR and CCPA which establish requirements and penalties for non-compliance. It’s essential for organizations to use privacy risk assessment techniques to identify potential risks and develop strategies to mitigate them, as data privacy is a critical issue in today’s digital age and the correct protection techniques must be applied.

The GDPR enshrines the right of every individual to receive clear and understandable explanations about the logic behind automated decision-making processes. Also to see if it has biases and for create trust in the AI system.

There is a field of AI called Explainable AI which uses surrogate models that imitate the behaviour of the black box explaining it. We have developed a framework in order to explain any type of model on any domain.

The privacy risk of a machine learning model is computed with a particular attack called Membership Inference Attack. Once we establish the level of risk of the model we apply a mitigation strategy in order to keep the performances of the model and reducing the privacy risk.
